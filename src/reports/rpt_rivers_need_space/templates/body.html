This Riverscapes Need Space Report is an estimate of how much space the riverscape could utilize, based on models of valley bottom (riverscapes) from the Valley Bottom Extraction Tool driven by a 10 m NED digital elevation dataset and the National Hydrography Dataset (CONUS2025 Run). The model and this report are only as good as the underlying data used to generate them, but they can help begin to characterize intrinsic riverscape properties for your riverscape, as well as start to consider current conditions from the perspective of the first principle of healthy riverscapes - healthy riverscapes need access to their valley bottoms.

Appendix 2 - Source Data

This report was generated using data from the Riverscapes Data Exchange. Free, publicly accessible data were processed through a series of models by the Riverscapes Consortium, culminating with the Riverscapes Metric Engine (RME) that synthesizes metrics together for all the preceding models. These RME data are based on polygons that span the valley bottom and are segmented at a distance that varies with drainage area. These polygons, or Discrete Geographic Objects (DGOs) possess over 100 metrics spanning a variety of topics pertaining to riverscapes health.

The model runs in question were all part of an initiative to produce a continuous riverscapes dataset for the Conterminous United States in 2025.
