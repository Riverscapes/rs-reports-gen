The riverscapes inventory report provides insight into the current state and health of riverine ecosystems. It pulls together data from a variety of public sources and combines them with the results from several models covering a range of topics from valley bottom identification, beaver restoration and riparian condition. The goal is to provide a broad overview of the riverscape and the landscape context in which it resides.

Stream Order

Stream order is a measure of the relative size of streams. It is determined by the hierarchy of tributaries in a drainage basin. In this report, we use the Strahler method to classify stream order, where the smallest tributaries are assigned an order of 1. When two streams of the same order merge, the resulting stream is assigned an order that is one higher. If streams of different orders merge, the resulting stream retains the higher order.

Appendix 2 - Source Data

This report was generated using data from the Riverscapes Data Exchange. Free, pubicly accessibable data were processed through a series of models by the Riverscapes Consortium, culminating with the Riverscapes Metric Engine (RME) that synthesizes metrics together for all the preceeding models. These RME data are based on polygons that span the valley bottom and are segmented at a distance that varies with drainage area. These polygons, or discreet geographic objects (DGOs) possess over 100 metrics spanning a variety of topics pertaining to riverscape health.

The model runs in question were all part of an initiative to produce a continuous riverscapes dataset for the Conterminous United States in 2025.
